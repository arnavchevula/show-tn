# Growth Notes

## How Long Until Google Indexes the Site?

**Basic indexing:** 1–4 weeks for the homepage to show up if submitted manually through Google Search Console. Without submission it can take months.

**Actually ranking for anything:** 3–6 months minimum, and that's with consistent traffic and some inbound links. "Chicago live music" is a competitive keyword — up against TimeOut, DoStuff, Bandsintown, and the Chicago Reader who have years of domain authority.

### What to Do at Launch
- Submit your sitemap to Google Search Console immediately — don't wait for Google to find you organically
- Get one or two inbound links from real sites early (a local blog, Reddit post in r/chicago, a venue sharing your link) — signals to Google the site is legitimate
- The structured data (JSON-LD Event schema) helps Google understand the content and can surface shows in rich results faster than normal rankings

### The Honest Reality
SEO will be slow. The faster path to early traffic is:
- Post in **r/chicago** and Chicago Facebook groups
- Get a single venue to share it to their audience — one mid-size venue with 10k Instagram followers posting "check out this site listing our shows" will drive more traffic in a day than a month of SEO

## Monetization

### Ticket Affiliate Links
The most natural fit. When linking to a show, use affiliate links through **Dice**, **Ticketmaster**, or **SeatGeek** — they all have affiliate programs. You're already sending people to ticket pages, you'd just earn a small cut. Zero friction for the user.

### Featured / Promoted Listings
Charge venues to pin their show to the top of listings or highlight it. Small Chicago venues already spend money on Instagram ads — this is a more targeted audience. **$50–200/month per venue** is realistic at small scale.

### Email Newsletter
A weekly "here's what's worth seeing this week in Chicago" email. Once you have a list, that's sponsorable. Local bars, restaurants, and venues near the music scene would pay to be mentioned. Low overhead, high trust if the curation is good.

### City Expansion
Once the model works in Chicago, the same scraper architecture works in any city. More cities = more traffic = more leverage for any of the above.

### What to Avoid
- **Display ads** (Google AdSense etc.) — pay almost nothing at small scale and make the site look cheap
- **Paywalling listings** — kills the core value proposition
